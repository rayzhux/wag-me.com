# WAG Corporate Website - Origin Story & History

> This file contains the narrative arc for WAG's history. The story should center on WAG as its own entity, with the founder's track record as credibility proof -- not as a parent-company dependency.

## The Full Timeline

### 1978 - The Beginning
Amin Kadrie relocates to the Middle East, joining Alghanim Industries in Kuwait as Group VP Automotive. Alghanim was one of GM's largest distributors outside the US. Amin eventually became CEO of Alghanim Industries, achieving 42% GM market share in the country.

### 1993 - First Dealership Acquisition
Amin acquires Lexus of London Ontario in Canada -- the beginning of what becomes the Phaeton Automotive Group. Lexus of London became the only Lexus dealer in Canada with market share equal to or greater than all competitors.

### 1996 - Middle East Expansion
Operations expand to the Middle East, establishing management consulting and automotive advisory in Dubai.

### 2002-2013 - Canadian Dealership Excellence
Dealership operations in Canada grow to 1st-percentile performance metrics. The portfolio includes Richmond Hill Toyota, Richmond Hill & Thornhill Hyundai, and Lexus of London Ontario -- leading dealerships in the Toronto area, recognized for operational excellence. When eventually sold, the Canadian operations achieved a 22x exit multiple.

### 2004 - FastTrack Launched
FastTrack launches as an automotive services joint venture with Emarat at fuel stations across the UAE. Over the next 20 years, FastTrack becomes one of the longest-serving quick service providers in the UAE, growing to 32+ locations and serving 330,000+ customers.

### 2010 - AutoData ME / Vicimus
AutoData Middle East is established -- pioneering vehicle data, policy issuance, claims management, and vehicle history reporting solutions in the GCC. Built on proprietary regional datasets and global partnerships with JATO Dynamics and CARFAX. Vicimus launches in Canada as an automotive software solutions platform for dealers.

### 2013-2023 - Regional Scaling
- National Rental (Saudi Arabia): Transformed from 300 cars to 28,000 cars
- Hyundai UAE: Grew from 300 cars to 12,000 annual sales, later sold in 2023
- Toyota Lexus dealership: Exited in 2021, providing the capital to fund the WAG vision

### 2024 - World Automotive Group Takes Shape
The decision is made to consolidate all automotive businesses into a single platform company -- World Automotive Group. Strategy, leadership recruitment, and operational integration begin.

### 2025 - Incorporated and Launched
WAG is formally incorporated at ADGM (Abu Dhabi Global Market) as World Automotive Group International Holdings Limited on 23 September 2025 -- a Private Company Limited By Shares (Registered Number 31285). A new professional leadership team assembles under CEO Terence Johnsson: global automotive executives from Audi, VW, GM, Procter & Gamble, TotalEnergies, and technology leaders from across China, North America, and the Middle East. The mandate: take 40 years of hard-won expertise and rebuild it as a technology-driven platform company with a venture studio at its core.

## The Narrative Arc (for Website Storytelling)

### From Expertise to Platform

WAG's roots run deep -- over 40 years of automotive leadership built by Chairman Amin Kadrie across Kuwait, Canada, Saudi Arabia, and the UAE. The track record is real: 42% GM market share in Kuwait, a 22x exit multiple in Canada, scaling from 300 rental cars to 28,000, building 12,000 annual Hyundai sales from scratch. FastTrack grew to 32+ locations across the UAE. AutoData became the region's leading independent vehicle data provider.

But WAG isn't a legacy business. In 2025, the automotive portfolio was formally consolidated into a new entity -- World Automotive Group International Holdings Limited -- incorporated at ADGM with its own leadership and its own ambition. A new professional team was assembled under CEO Terence Johnsson: operators and builders from Audi, VW, GM, Procter & Gamble, TotalEnergies, AXA, and technology companies across China, North America, and the Middle East.

The mandate: take deep domain expertise and rebuild it as a technology-driven, entrepreneurial platform company.

Today, WAG operates with a startup mindset and institutional discipline:
- A Venture Studio to launch and scale new businesses
- AI and data at the core of every product and operating decision
- Small teams, fast cycles, real ownership -- no bureaucracy, no legacy process
- A clear ambition: build the technology backbone of the automotive economy across MENA and beyond

The past gave us expertise. The present is about building something entirely new with it.

## Key Narrative Themes for Website

1. **Expertise as foundation, not identity:** 40+ years of proven results are the launchpad, not the destination. WAG is forward-looking.
2. **New entity, new leadership, new ambition:** WAG was incorporated at ADGM in 2025 with its own team and its own strategy. This is not a rebrand of something old -- it's a new company built on proven foundations.
3. **Operator DNA:** WAG is built by people who have run dealerships, scaled service networks, and shipped products -- not just consultants or financial engineers.
4. **Entrepreneurial culture:** Venture studio at the core. Small teams. Real ownership. Anyone can propose an idea.
5. **Conviction capital:** Funded by decades of successful exits, not outside money chasing trends.
6. **Proof in numbers:** Every claim has a track record behind it (42% market share, 22x exit, 300 to 28,000 cars).
